# FYEC100 Knowledge Base

## Course Overview

FYEC100 is a first-year elective course designed to help new COSTAATT students transition into college-level learning. The course introduces academic expectations, student success habits, digital learning tools, reflective practice, and academic integrity.

## Common Student Questions

### What is FYEC100 about?

FYEC100 helps first-year students build confidence and readiness for higher education. Students learn how to manage time, participate in classes, use the LMS, communicate professionally, reflect on their learning, and understand academic integrity.

### What are students expected to do?

Students are expected to attend and participate in scheduled learning activities, review course materials in the LMS, complete assignments by the stated deadlines, communicate respectfully, and seek help early when they are uncertain.

### How should students approach assignments?

Students should read the assignment instructions carefully, identify the task, review the rubric where available, make a simple outline, draft in their own words, cite sources when required, and submit through the LMS by the deadline.

### Can the assistant write assignments?

No. The assistant may explain instructions, suggest an outline, help students brainstorm responsibly, review general expectations, and encourage revision. It must not write full submissions for students.

### Can the assistant grade work?

No. The assistant must not grade work, assign marks, or predict official scores. Students should use lecturer feedback, the rubric, and official course guidance.

## Academic Integrity Guidance

Academic integrity means completing work honestly, acknowledging sources, and submitting work that reflects the student's own learning. Students should not copy from websites, classmates, or AI tools and present that work as their own. When unsure, students should ask their lecturer how AI tools may be used for a specific assignment.

## Study Tips

- Review the LMS course shell at least twice each week.
- Keep a weekly study calendar with class time, reading time, assignment work, and revision time.
- Break assignments into smaller steps: understand, plan, draft, revise, proofread, submit.
- Ask questions early instead of waiting until the deadline.
- Save copies of drafts and submission confirmations.

## LMS Navigation Guidance

Students should use the LMS to locate announcements, the course outline, weekly modules, readings, assignment instructions, discussion activities, grades or feedback where enabled, and submission links. If a student cannot access the course shell, cannot see a submission link, or has a technical login issue, they should contact the LMS administrator or official student support channel.

## Escalation Guidance

Students should contact the lecturer when they need clarification on assignment instructions, course content, deadlines, grading criteria, or feedback. Students should contact the LMS administrator when they have technical issues such as login problems, missing course access, upload errors, or trouble finding LMS tools.

## Fallback Guidance

If a question is not covered in this knowledge base, the assistant should say that the information is not available in the Phase 1 knowledge base and refer the student to the lecturer, course outline, LMS, or LMS administrator.
